DEVLOG - Fun-damentals
03/22/24

video games are supposed to be fun. zombie game is an experience in roblox terms. experience is a game. every game has their own way to keep it fun and fresh for their players, and here's how (hopefully) zombie game can keep being fun for the player.

1 - INCENTIVE
zombie game is extremely repetitive, the gameplay loop, simply put, being "kill zombies and don't die. if you die then turn into a zombie then kill humans and don't die" needs some interesting factors to make it more lively.
a rather important part of that gameplay loop is giving players a stop watch that checks how long they've survived in the world before turning into a zombie. that time is the Survival Time. if the player is about to turn into a zombie, they have a chance to add extra time onto their survival time by attempting to kill survivors. time added onto the survival time is called Final Time. the final time is then sent to a leaderboard where players can compare their scores. the final time should also challenge players to see how far they can go. basically:
if you're a survivor, survive for as long as you can for that juicy long survival time.
if you're a zombie, unleash your rage for that juicy final time bonus.

killing zombies should also be fun. gore should be satisfying (if i add any) and it should be relieving to land a hit on a basic zombie like a shambler. making npc zombies easier to kill? maybe? idk

2 - EXPERIENCE
every time i boot up G&B, it's for 3 reasons:
1 - because i want Old Guard
2 - because killing zombies is fun
3 - because seeing people die in stupid ways is fun..ny
the 3rd reason is very important in adding that experience. wacky or serious things happening during gameplay can really emphasize intensity.

imagine, for a moment, carlhecox2003, a random that has helped you throughout the game, giving you weapons and never talking once during those interactions, accidentally alerts a horde. you and carlhecox2003 defend the shop you've called your base with your lives. unfortunately, carlhecox2003 goes too close to the horde and gets eaten alive. you have no choice but to run away as you see his corpse arise, joining the horde's ranks.

sounds pretty damn serious, huh? hopefully this is a recurring scenario that happens all the time in zombie game, along with goofy shit like people falling off of skyscrapers due to large amounts of hordes, etc etc. it just should be a perfect mix of wacky and serious scenarios that should keep a player playing the game.

3 - REPLAYABILITY
we just talked about this in fundamental 1, but replayability deserves its own fundamental. zombie game should be interesting enough to be played over and over again without a dull experience. one solution is an idea that could be a little outlandish, but could also work. random generation. every server is a different town with a different set of buildings and such. added with extra factors (won't spoil ha ha), every server should have a different experience. although this disallows players to plan ahead, it hopefully should just encourage them to improve on skill as a survivor or zombie in general, rather than muscle memory. this is just a concept though.
a simple cheap stupid dumb idiot way is badges. i have a shit ton of ideas for badges, and some of them are really fun! spoiler alert: there is 1 badge with 6 absurd requirements. the rest of the badges should be fine though.
if i end up doing both of these or atleast just one, hopefully it helps increase replayability.

and those are the fundamental requirements. what's important about these fundaments is that they should compliment eachother in order to give the player a good fun zombie experience killing zombies with hatchets and whatnot. that's all i really have to say that's the fundamentals yay
